- The active conceptual architecture is:
  - Learning activities explains the activity types and course mechanics.
  - Evaluation explains how grades are calculated.
  - Course policies contains enforceable rules and consequences.
- Current evaluation rules:
  - Two homework assignments = 40% of final grade.
  - Two partial exams = 30% of final grade.
  - One final exam = 30% of final grade.
  - H1, H2, E1, and E2 each combine Written component 20% and Oral component 80%.
  - E_F has no Oral component and no coevaluation.
  - DataCamp certifications add +10 points to the next scheduled exam when completed on time.
  - Wheel-of-fortune bonus points apply to the next scheduled exam unless stated otherwise.
  - Bonus points are individual, not shared.
  - Coevaluation is based on the average score assigned by teammates.
  - There are two coevaluations: first for H1/E1, second for H2/E2.
  - If a student does not submit a coevaluation, they lose the opportunity to evaluate teammates.
  - If nobody in a team submits a coevaluation, that coevaluation step is skipped; marks are not adjusted by coevaluation, and this does not mean students lose points.
  - A low coevaluation can make a student fail an activity even if the group mark was good.

- Course policies keeps the enforceable Campus Contribution evidence, conduct, timing, consent, and absence consequences.
- Final exam has no required physical classroom presence and can be completed remotely according to Schedule/policies.
